Frequently Asked Questions

For employee

What is Plutus?

Our mission is to empower startup employees to exercise their ESOPs. While receiving employee stock options should be a life-changing financial opportunity, the value of these options remains inaccessible and illiquid until an exit event. In India, employees face a short exercise period during which they must arrange substantial liquidity to accommodate taxes, which is considered deemed income for the employee. We're here to address this problem by enabling employees to secure funding from investors to exercise their ESOPs. Investors, who seek to invest in unlisted startup companies based on their potential, gain an early entry into the market at lower rates to maximize their profits. This, in turn, provides employees with the ability to create liquidity for some of their ESOPs.

How does the platform work?

We accept funding requests from employees of unlisted startups for their ESOPs. These requests are presented as investment opportunities, including a detailed study of the company's performance, to potential investors. Investors then provide funding to employees. In exchange, an agreement is signed wherein employees commit to sharing profits with the participating investors following a successful exit event.

Why should I use Plutus?

Plutus provides you with the funding you need to exercise your options. ESOPs can be a great source of future income, but they are often challenging to utilize. Companies typically offer employees a very limited window of time to exercise their options, during which they must secure liquidity to cover taxes. As a result, many employees are forced to forego their ESOP options.

We offer employees the opportunity to generate liquidity for their ESOPs by providing funding through investors, without the need to wait for an exit event. With Plutus, you will always retain sole ownership of your shares and will receive the profits from the exit event without having to pay taxes.

What documents do I need to provide?

To initiate the process, you will be required to provide us with your basic details, including proof of identity and your company email ID for verification purposes. Additionally, you will need to submit your ESOP grant letter, disclose your option agreement status, and provide the latest Fair Market Value (FMV).

After you have received funding, you will be asked to provide certain government-issued identifications and demonstrate that you have exercised your options by linking your demat account.

How does Plutus decide whether to fund me?

First, we conduct a thorough background research on you using the provided details, and your documents are manually verified by our legal team to assess your credibility. Once you are approved, we present funding opportunities to our investors, and it is ultimately their decision whether to fund your stock options.

what will happen after funding?

Once you receive funding, you will exercise your stock options, and you will be required to provide evidence of the exercise by linking your demat account. In accordance with the agreement clause signed before funding, you are obligated to keep us updated on any relevant information you receive regarding any potential exit event.

When will I be able to sell my share?

Once exercised you remain the sole shareholder. As such, you will be able to sell your shares during any relevant liquidity event that the company offers.

What happens after an exit event?

An exit event typically comprises four main scenarios: an initial public offering (IPO), a merger or acquisition, a buyback of company shares, or the sale of shares, as well as any other event within your company that enables shareholders to cash out some or all of their shares.

Following a successful exit, you will be obligated to repay the funding amount you received to the investors and share a portion of the profits with them as per the prior agreement.

However, if there is no exit or if your company goes out of business, you will not be required to make any payments to the investors.

Will there be any risks associated with Investing through Plutus?

The valuation of startups can be subjective and speculative. The value of your investment is often tied to the company's valuation, which can change significantly over time. Investments made through Plutus may not be easily liquidated. Unlike publicly traded stocks, it can be challenging to sell private company shares, and it may take years to see a return on your investment, if at all.

What is an Exit Event and what happens after it?

An exit event typically comprises four main scenarios: an initial public offering (IPO), a merger or acquisition, a buyback of company shares, or the sale of shares, as well as any other event within your company that enables shareholders to cash out some or all of their shares. After a successful Exit event, you'll not only get your initial investment back but also a share of the earnings. The employee is obligated to provide you with your share of the earnings in cash shortly after the conclusion of any relevant lockup period.

How do you ensure the employee does not Default?

Employees are legally bound to complete the transaction promptly once a successful liquidity event occurs. However, when funding employee stock options, there is a risk that the employee may fail to honor their contractual obligations. In the event of such a breach, we would pursue legal action to compel the employee to adhere to the terms of the contract.
